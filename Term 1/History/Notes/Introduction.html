3 Avoiding Bias in Japanese History

Japanese history itself has a history. We see a movement from glorifying it as part of the Orient (like, from the 1200’s on, people thought it was a fantasyland), to comparing it favorably to England after the Meiji Restoration. There’s a lot of history that glorifies it militarily after the Russo-Japanese war, and demonizes it in the 1930’s and then as a prime example of modernization, especially in the 1980’s. There hae been criticisms of this, though. Reischauer’s “incipient capitalism” theory says that we need to use Japan as a model for underdeveloped Asian nations, but this drive is really driven by capitalism.
